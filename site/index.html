📈 Progressive Overload

Without progression, results stop. Follow the 2-for-2 rule.

"If you complete 2 extra reps on the last set for 2 sessions in a row — increase the weight by 2–2.5 kg"

Hair & Grooming

Short textured buzz · Clean neckline · Maintenance every 2–3 weeks

Your Recommended Cut

Short Textured Buzz / Crop

At this length, the grey/dark contrast reads as distinguished, not unkempt. Your defined facial structure becomes the focus — which is exactly where the attention should be. Requires a barber visit or home clipper every 2–3 weeks. Zero daily styling time.

Barber Brief — Copy & Show Your Barber

BARBER BRIEF Cut: Short textured crop / buzz On top: Grade 3–4 (~10–12mm), slight texture if scissors available Sides: Grade 1–2 taper fade — blend into top Neckline: Clean tapered neckline — not squared, slightly tapered Blend: Low to mid fade on sides, blended into top Beard: Clean all over at Grade 1 (stubble) OR close shave Neckline: two finger-widths above Adam's apple, hard line Cheekline: natural, cleaned up — no shaping Product: Light matte paste or nothing — avoid shine products

Beard Options

OptionLookEffort
Clean shaveSharp, emphasises jaw structureEvery 2–3 days
Short stubble (Grade 1–2)Distinguished, adds jaw definitionTrim every 3–4 days
Grey beard is an asset at 55 — do not dye it. Clean neckline is the only rule.

Daily Grooming (5 minutes)

Daily: Moisturiser + SPF on face. Check beard line.
Every 2–3 days: Shave/trim beard to chosen length. Clean neckline.
Every 2–3 weeks: Barber or home clipper cut. Don't let it grow out — the effect depends on staying sharp.
