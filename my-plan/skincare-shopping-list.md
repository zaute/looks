# Your Personal Skincare Shopping List
**Profile:** True Winter — cool undertone, fair skin, grey-blue eyes, redness-prone (nose + face)
**Strategy:** Build the routine in stages. Don't buy everything at once — introduce one product at a time.

---

## Stage 1 — Week 1 (Buy Now)
Get these first. They give the biggest immediate impact.

### SPF 50 — Daily, last step every morning
| Tier | Product | Price | Why |
|------|---------|-------|-----|
| Budget | Altruist Face Fluid SPF 50 | ~£2 | Lightweight, non-greasy, great value |
| Mid | La Roche-Posay Anthelios Uvmune 400 Fluid SPF 50+ | ~£20 | Specifically formulated for sensitive/redness-prone skin |
| Premium | Ultrasun Face SPF 50+ Anti-Pigmentation | ~£30 | Also targets uneven tone |

**Buy this first.** Nothing else matters until SPF is in the routine.

### Niacinamide Moisturiser — AM + PM after cleansing
| Tier | Product | Price | Why |
|------|---------|-------|-----|
| Budget | The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1% (serum) + CeraVe Moisturising Cream | ~£12 total | Use serum first, then moisturiser |
| Mid | Paula's Choice 10% Niacinamide Booster | ~£38 | Excellent formulation, reduces redness fast |
| Premium | SkinCeuticals Metacell Renewal B3 | ~£90 | Niacinamide + peptides in one |

## Stage 2 — Week 2–3 (Add These)
Once SPF and moisturiser are daily habits.

### Vitamin C Serum — AM only, before moisturiser
| Tier | Product | Price | Why |
|------|---------|-------|-----|
| Budget | The Ordinary Ascorbyl Glucoside Solution 12% | ~£9 | Stable, gentle form — good for sensitive/redness-prone |
| Mid | Timeless 20% Vitamin C + E + Ferulic Acid | ~£20 | Near-identical to SkinCeuticals at fraction of cost |
| Premium | SkinCeuticals C E Ferulic | ~£160 | The gold standard — worth it if budget allows |

### BHA Cleanser — 2–3x per week (PM cleanse only)
Used to smooth skin texture, reduce congestion on nose and beard area.
| Tier | Product | Price | Why |
|------|---------|-------|-----|
| Budget | The Ordinary Salicylic Acid 2% Anhydrous Solution | ~£5 | Apply after cleansing, not as cleanser |
| Mid | Paula's Choice Skin Perfecting 2% BHA Liquid | ~£35 | The best-tested BHA exfoliant available |
| Premium | (same as mid — Paula's Choice is already the premium standard here) | — | — |

⚠️ **Never use BHA on the same night as Retinol.** Alternate them.

---

## Stage 3 — Month 2 (Add This)
Only add retinol once redness is under control and skin barrier is stable.

### Retinol — PM only, 2x per week to start
| Tier | Product | Price | Why |
|------|---------|-------|-----|
| Budget | The Ordinary Retinol 0.2% in Squalane | ~£8 | Start here — lowest strength, tolerability-first |
| Mid | RoC Retinol Correxion Line Smoothing Serum | ~£25 | Encapsulated retinol, gentler delivery |
| Premium | SkinCeuticals Retinol 0.3 | ~£75 | Start at 0.3 for 55+ skin |

---

## Your Full Daily Routine (once all products introduced)

**AM:**
1. Lukewarm water rinse
2. Vitamin C serum — wait 60 seconds
3. Niacinamide serum (if using separately)
4. Moisturiser
5. SPF 50 ← **never skip**

**PM:**
1. Oil cleanser or micellar water
2. Gentle cleanser (or BHA cleanser 2–3x/week)
3. Retinol (on non-BHA nights, 2x/week to start)
4. Heavy moisturiser
