# Your Hair & Grooming Plan
**Based on analysis:** Short buzz cut, clean beard neckline, skin-close shave or uniform length

---

## What the Analysis Found
- Dark remaining hair with visible grey/salt-and-pepper
- Hairline receding (or short by choice)
- Beard area needs a clean neckline to frame the jaw
- **Recommendation:** Embrace the short look — it reads intentional, not compensating

---

## Your Recommended Cut
**The Short Textured Buzz / Crop**

Why this works for you:
- Receding hairline looks **intentional at uniform length** — never like you're hiding it
- Grey/dark contrast at this length reads as distinguished, not unkempt
- Requires maintenance every **2–3 weeks** to stay sharp (not monthly — the tidy look is the look)
- Zero styling time daily

### Maintenance at home:
Between barber visits, a clipper at consistent grade keeps it looking deliberate. Consistency of length is the whole point.

---

## Barber Brief
Copy this and show it to your barber:

```
BARBER BRIEF

Cut: Short textured crop / buzz
On top: Grade 3–4 (approximately 10–12mm), slight texture if scissors available
Sides: Grade 1–2 taper fade — blend into top
Neckline: Clean tapered neckline — not squared, slightly tapered
Blend: Low to mid fade on sides, blended into top
Beard: Clean all over at Grade 1 (stubble) OR close shave
       Neckline: two finger-widths above Adam's apple, hard line
       Cheekline: natural, cleaned up — no shaping
Product: Light matte paste or nothing — avoid shine products
```

---

## Beard Options
You have two clean choices — both work:

| Option | What it looks like | Effort |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| **Clean shave** | Sharp, clean, emphasises jaw structure | Every 2–3 days |
| **Short stubble (Grade 1–2)** | Distinguished, adds jaw definition | Trim every 3–4 days |

**Avoid:** Anything longer without a very clean neckline — patchy or unchecked beard growth reads as neglect, not style.

Grey beard is an asset at 55 — do not dye it.

---

## Grooming Routine (5 minutes/day)
Pair with your skincare routine — happens in the same bathroom window.

**Daily:**
- Moisturiser + SPF on face (see skincare plan)
- Check beard line — does it need a trim?

**Every 2–3 days:**
- Shave beard to chosen length
- Clean up neckline with razor or trimmer

**Every 2–3 weeks:**
- Barber or home clipper cut to maintain length
- Don't let it grow out — the whole effect depends on staying sharp

| Use | Budget | Mid |

---

## Why This Works
A short, maintained cut at uniform length does three things:
1. **Removes the distraction** of uneven growth, receding patches, or unchecked grey — the eye reads clean lines as intentional
2. **Frames the face** — your jaw and eye structure (what the analysis called "defined facial structure") becomes the focus
3. **Pairs with the colour palette** — the high-contrast True Winter palette is even more striking with a clean, sharp head shape
